ESP32 IoT PCB

This custom IoT development board showcases the power of KiCad 9 RC1 and creates a versatile platform for real-world IoT projects.

Overview

At its heart is the ESP32-C3 microcontroller, which offers Wi-Fi and BLE connectivity along with impressive processing power. It’s compact, efficient, and ready for your next IoT application.

Project Details

The board supports multiple power options. It can be powered via a USB-C connector (which also handles data transfer) or a LiPo battery for portable setups. A dedicated battery management circuit ensures safe charging and discharging. Built-in 3.3V and 5V regulators provide stable voltage rails.

For storage, the board includes a microSD card slot for local data logging and SPI flash memory for storing configuration files, sensor data, or firmware updates.

The board integrates several sensors for environmental and audio monitoring:

  • BME280 for temperature, humidity, and pressure
  • Ambient light sensor for illumination monitoring
  • Microphone with pre-amplifier for sound capture

Connectivity is supported through I²C and SPI interfaces, making it compatible with a wide range of devices and peripherals. A USB-to-UART bridge simplifies programming and debugging.

The user interface includes boot and reset buttons, a GPIO header for external connections, and status LEDs for power, charging, and operation.

The PCB itself is a four-layer design with dedicated power and ground planes to maintain signal integrity and minimize electromagnetic interference (EMI). Test points are included to monitor signals and aid debugging. ESD protection components (e.g., TVS diodes) were placed near the USB and I/O interfaces to protect the microcontroller and sensors from voltage spikes, highlighting the importance of robust hardware design for real-world IoT devices.

What I Learned

  • Signal Integrity: Understanding return paths, controlled impedance, and careful layout to prevent noise or reflections for high-speed signals.
  • ESD Protection: Learned how to protect the board from voltage spikes by adding ESD protection.
  • PCB Stackup Decisions: Gained experience designing the PCB stackup for high-speed signals, understanding that placing ground planes close to signal layers improves return paths, reduces noise, and minimizes EMI.

Summary of Features

  • Microcontroller: ESP32-C3 with Wi-Fi and BLE
  • Power: USB-C input, LiPo battery connector, onboard 3.3V & 5V regulation
  • Storage: MicroSD card slot, SPI flash memory
  • Sensors: BME280 (environment), ambient light, microphone + pre-amplifier
  • Interfaces: I²C, SPI, USB-to-UART bridge
  • User Interaction: Boot/reset buttons, GPIO header, status LEDs
  • Design: 4-layer PCB, optimized layout, test points for debugging
