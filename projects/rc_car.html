RC Car Project

Custom-built 3D printed RC car with a fully designed PCB, SolidWorks chassis, and embedded control system

Overview

This project involved designing and building a fully custom remote-controlled (RC) car. The goal was to create a lightweight, stable, and high-performance vehicle while integrating electronics on a custom PCB. The chassis and controller housing were designed in SolidWorks and fabricated with 3D printing, combining mechanical design and electrical engineering into one system.

Key Features

  • Microcontroller: AVR128DB28 (responsible for motor control, wireless communication, and logic)
  • Motor Driver: L298N dual H-bridge motor driver for controlling DC motors
  • Chassis & Controller Housing: Designed in SolidWorks and 3D printed for lightweight strength and precise fit
  • Wireless Control: Communication module for remote operation
  • Object Detection: Distance-based sensors integrated for obstacle avoidance
  • Power Management: Designed for efficient current distribution and protection

Challenges

  • Balancing weight and stability in the SolidWorks-designed chassis
  • Designing a compact PCB that fit within the controller housing
  • Ensuring reliable motor driver performance under load
  • Integrating wireless control without interference

Learning Outcomes

  • Hands-on experience with PCB design and layout (signal routing, motor driver integration)
  • Practical understanding of embedded C programming for motor control
  • CAD design in SolidWorks for chassis and housing
  • Mechanical design considerations for stability and durability
  • System integration: combining electronics, power, and mechanical systems in one platform
