RC Car Project

Custom-built 3D printed RC car with custom PCB and wireless control

Team Members: Brayden Mitchell, Alexander Dollimount, Eric Ji, Dev Patel

Overview

This project involved designing and building a remote-controlled (RC) car featuring modular electronics, including distance sensing, a metal detector, and motor control. The car is wirelessly operated through a custom-built remote using RF communication. Mechanical and electrical systems were designed in parallel, with a custom 3D printed chassis, custom gearbox, and functional PCB layout, combining multiple disciplines into a working system.

Key Features

  • Microcontroller: AVR128DB28 (controls motors, servos, sensors, and LEDs)
  • Motor Driver: BTS7960 for main motor, VNH5019 for custom PCB testing
  • Motor: Tamiya 540 Sport Tuned Motor
  • Chassis & Controller Housing: Designed in SolidWorks and 3D printed
  • Wireless Control: TX118SA-4 transmitter and RX480-E4 receiver for RF communication
  • Distance Sensing: HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensor with LCD readout
  • Metal Detection: 555 timer-based metal detector triggering a buzzer
  • Power Management: 7.4V LiPo battery regulated to 5V for car electronics; AA 3.3V for controller

Challenges

  • Ensuring motor driver reliability under load; original L298N insufficient for motor
  • Implementing accurate distance sensing within PWM and AVR timer limitations
  • Managing RF interference and controller input timing
  • Mechanical issues like steering drift, low traction wheels, and heavy stainless steel axles

Learning Outcomes

  • Hands-on experience with PCB design, including high-current motor driver circuits
  • Embedded C programming for AVR microcontrollers to control motors, servos, and sensors
  • Mechanical design for stability, smooth operation, and compact PCB integration
  • Integration of multiple systems: electronics, mechanical design, and wireless control
  • Iterative testing and problem-solving in both hardware and software domains
